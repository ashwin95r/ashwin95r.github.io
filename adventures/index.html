Selected expeditions.

Alpine Lakes Wilderness, WA

The Enchantments Core Zone

Permit-limited backpacking in the Alpine Lakes Wilderness. The lottery is competitive; the terrain is worth the planning.

Olympic Peninsula, WA

Olympic Peninsula

Temperate rainforest, alpine terrain, and a dramatically different feel from the Cascades. The Hoh and Hurricane Ridge on the same peninsula.

BC · Alberta · California · Oregon · Washington

A winter on skis

This year covered a lot of ground: Banff, Lake Louise, Jasper, and Revelstoke in the Canadian Rockies; Mammoth and Palisades Tahoe in the Sierra Nevada; Mt Bachelor in Oregon; Crystal Mountain and Snoqualmie Pass close to home. Each mountain has its own character — Revelstoke's vertical, Mammoth's high-altitude light, Bachelor's uncrowded runs. Working on technique more than ticking names.

AIARE Level 1 · Pacific Northwest

Avalanche training — AIARE Level 1

Completed the AIARE Level 1 avalanche course this year. Snowpack assessment, terrain reading, rescue protocol, beacon/probe/shovel practice. A lot of it confirmed instincts already built on the trail — build an honest model of conditions, don't let objective beauty override objective hazard. Good preparation for the backcountry objectives on the list.

North Cascades National Park, WA

North Cascades — Multi-day Routes

The remote north. Fewer permits, longer approaches, less forgiving terrain. The kind of wilderness that requires actual navigation decisions, not just following a trail.

Blue Mountains, NSW, Australia

Blue Mountains, Sydney Years

The walks that introduced me to serious hiking while I was based in Sydney. Sandstone canyons, eucalyptus forest, dramatic valley lookouts. A different kind of beautiful than the Cascades, and a different kind of hard.

A note on why

Use challenge as information.

The mountains are an honest feedback system. Difficulty reveals something about the terrain — and something about you. A system under pressure tells you more than a system at rest.

Restraint is intelligence. Not every peak is meant to be climbed. Knowing when to turn back — before conditions force the decision — is a skill that takes longer to build than technical ability.

Fall in love with the process. Joy in the climb sustains you longer than any view.

Most of this applies to people and to life in general.
